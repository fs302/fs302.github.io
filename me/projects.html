One-World: Large-Scale Heterogenerous Graph in E-Commerce Platform

 

Related Techniques: Link Prediction, Community Detection

Graph is a powerful system to model the heterogenerous connections and interactions on online-shopping platform.

In order to gain a deeper understanding of our customers, we propose a tri-level identity resolution approach known as 'Device-Person-Household’.
Link prediction techniques like Resource-Allocation(Zhou et al., EPJ’09) and SEAL(Zhang et al., NeuIPS’18) are employed to establish missing connections and classify relations, resulting in an impressive collection of over 100 billion social relations with tagged labels.

InfoNet: Modeling Information Diffusion in Social Networks

 

Related Techniques: Graph Neural Networks, Social Recommendation

We have developed extensive social and knowledge graphs to comprehensively understand the relationships among device-user, user-user, item-item, and user-item. These graphs are leveraged to enhance recommendation algorithms and improve demand-side operations.

Our research has demonstrated that social relations on Taobao exhibit homophily, which can serve as an additional data source for user behavior modeling (Huang et al., SIGIR’21) and item sales modeling (Yang et al., TKDE’22).

To further boost the power of social relations in the recommendation, we employ graph structure learning(GSL) techniques on the social graph to generate superior recommendations for inactive users.[To Appear]
